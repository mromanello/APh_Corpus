Studies various aspects connected with the Lex Claudia, promulgated in 218 B.C. with the support of Gaius Flaminius, emphasizing the law-forming influence of the censor in the context of the « regimen morum », including Cicero's concept of the « honestum » and other virtues, the incompatibility of trade and « decorum », and the resistance on the part of the Roman nobility to Flaminius' reforms

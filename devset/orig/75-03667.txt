Although in ancient Christianity the ethical tenor of the parable of the Good Samaritan was not fully neglected, the parable was usually interpreted allegorically, the Samaritan being seen as Christ the Saviour of sinners, who had been robbed by the devil. Survey of the various interpretations and elaborations of the Church Fathers, including Irenaeus, Clement of Alexandria, Origen, Gregory Thaumaturgus, the Gospel of Philip, Ambrose, Augustine. In conclusion, some remarks on Christian charity in the first centuries C.E. which may in part have provided the hermeneutical context of the allegorization of the parable.

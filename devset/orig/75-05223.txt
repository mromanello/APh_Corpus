Had a capable general been in command of legions XVII, XVIII, and XVIIII in the Teutoburg Forest where Varus was defeated in A.D. 9, the disaster would in all likelihood not have occurred. A. Caecina Severus, a relatively minor figure in the « Annales », may serve as an exemplar of the sort of commander who was able to overcome « uirtutem hostium » and « naturam aduersam » (ann. 1, 63-72).

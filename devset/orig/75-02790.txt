Against Feldman (=> 59-02933), it is argued that Josephus does not emphasize Samson's wisdom, courage/bravery, and moderation ; neither does he defend Samson against the charge of injustice nor diminish the role of God so as to present him as a human hero. Rather, Josephus' image of Samson is akin to the one found in the Book of Judges, and the biblical Samson is hardly a virtuous character

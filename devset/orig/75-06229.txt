Although A. E. Housman's emendation (in CR 1905 19 : 260-261) of « exspectat » (in the sense of « moratur ») to « Hesperiam » at Aen. 4, 225 is elegant and gives excellent meaning, it could be improved on with « optatas ». Aen. 4, 225-226 thus reads : « qui nunc/optatas fatisque datas non respicit urbes ». The lines remind us of Aeneas' ardent wish (« optatas ») while also alluding to what is the highest will (« fatis datas »). There must be harmony between Aeneas' longing for a national existence and what is granted him by Fate.

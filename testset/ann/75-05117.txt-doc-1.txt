The significant import of chthonic elements, especially chthonic deities, in Sophocles ' « Oedipus Coloneus » has been widely noticed and variously discussed.
The focus of this discussion traced, however, historical cult and religion without regard to a possible literary aspect.
Here it is shown how Sophocles throughout the play intentionally associates his protagonist with the sphere of the chthonic and dark as a means of creating a symbolic language and as a device of personal characterization.

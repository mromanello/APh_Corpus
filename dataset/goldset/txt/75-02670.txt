When dealing with the alternatives between tradition and the individual talent, the « Ars » in 119-27 sets them up as the two ways of providing poetic (i.e. epic or tragic) material. In 128-30, the difficulty of being individual and original, as regards the creation of a new tradition by the poet himself, is brought forward - « propria communiter dicere » in 128 means « by yourself to say something that will become common property » - and it is recommended that the poet stick to tradition. Finally, in 131-52, wrong and right ways of handling traditional epic material are pointed out, which leads to a concluding praise of Homer

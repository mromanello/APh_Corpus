The fact that « uocabulum » appears with far more frequency in Tacitus ' texts than in any other author except the encyclopaedists argues for his idiosyncratic usage of the term.
Words that Tacitus designates as « uocabula » are focal points around which the narrative of empire arranges itself.
Examination of examples (e. g., ann. 1, 3, 7 and Germ. 2, 3) outlines what, according to Tacitus, were the significant developments in the way people thought and spoke about themselves after the onset of the Julio-Claudian regime.

In Plato 's R. 6, « Meno », « Lysis », « Alcibiades » and « Theages », and Xenophon 's Mem. 4, Socrates is portrayed as pushing his interlocutors to admit that their ambition is tyrannical and amorally self-sufficient.
Socrates offers them a way to get control of their destinies by abandoning their ambition.

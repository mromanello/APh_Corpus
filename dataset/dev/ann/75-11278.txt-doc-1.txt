The Northwest Iberian Peninsula increased its contacts both with the Mediterranean and with the Atlantic from the end of the 2nd millennium B. C. onward.
The fall of the Phoenician colonies and the Roman conquest of the West affected this marginal geographical location.
Ceramic finds indicate that Atlantic contacts increased from the late-2nd cent.
B. C. to reach a climax under Roman rule in the 1st and 2nd cents.
A. D. The Roman presence favored more intense relations between the Atlantic regions.
Hispanic amphorae in France and Britain and black-burnished pottery found in Asturias and northern Galicia attest to these relationships.

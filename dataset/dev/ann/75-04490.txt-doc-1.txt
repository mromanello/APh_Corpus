Examination of the consolatory tradition and of precedents for grief over the death of a child shed light on Plutarch 's reasons for writing a « consolatio » on the death of his infant daughter (Moralia 608 A-612 B), and for publishing this formal consolation instead of comforting his wife more personally.
Plutarch was comforting himself while both celebrating his wife and erecting a monument to his child by the best means he was master of, literature.

From 1997 to 2002, the British School at Rome 's archaeological program has been the Tiber Valley Project, studying the changing landscape of the middle Tiber valley from proto-history to the medieval period.
It has focused on the impact of the growth, success, and transformation of Rome on the history of the settlement and economy of the river valley.
This report focuses on the results of the restudy of the South Etruria Survey material in order reevaluate earlier interpretations.
In particular, Rome 's role is considered with regard to three south Etrurian crises : the late 5th and early 4th cents.

The critical tradition of the « Itinerarium », since the discovery of its manuscript in 1884, may be considered as having been implictly concerned with the unanswered question of the typological determination of the text.
Here, the major traditional positions that may prove relevant in more precisely circumscribing the text are sketched.
Rather than merely reflecting a conflicting terminology, its various designations seem to indicate the necessity of redescribing a text which apparently defies our traditional categories

The « Descriptiones » of Callistratus is mediated by the aesthetic experience and performance practice of the pantomime stage.
Early modern ways of coupling theatrical « mimesis » with the plastic arts share with ancient pantomime the art of building character and passion through a corporeal medium as well as the experience of bridging the statuesqueness of the plastic arts and the narrative of the dramatic stage, a blend that was a distinctive feature of ancient pantomime dancing.

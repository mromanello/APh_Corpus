Studies some subordinating conjunctions and modal lexemes among the instances concerned with grammaticalisation in Latin, sharing the common element « licet », which underwent two processes of grammaticalisation, when it became a modal verb and a subordinating concessive conjunction.
Topics studied include the relationship between grammaticalisation and correlation (« cum ... tum »), the freezing of an inflectional form of the relative pronoun (« quod », « quia ») ; the historical development of concessive conjunctions (« licet », « quamuis », « quamquam »), modal verbs (« licet », « debeo », « possum », « necesse est », « queo ») and modal adverbs (« nimirum »).
These concessive and modal lexemes derive respectively from non-concessive and non-modal expressions.

From 1997 to 1999 Kherson State Pedagogical University, along with a small team from the University of Manitoba, continued its excavation of Belozerskoe in southern Ukraine, located at the mouth of the Dnieper.
The field seasons of 1997-1999 focused on the eastern part of the settlement and the results have shown that the site is suitable for long-term archaeological study despite significant disturbance of the cultural layer.
Three major building periods can be distinguished from the second quarter of the 4th cent.
B. C. to the first quarter of the 3rd cent.
B. C. The settlement of Belozerskoe is set apart by the distinctive nature of certain household structures, by the variety of the finds, and by the relative prosperity of its inhabitants.

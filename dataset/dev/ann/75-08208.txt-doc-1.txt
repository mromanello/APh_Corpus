On the contexts of burned faunal assemblages from C. W. Blegen 's excavations at the Palace of Nestor (=> APh 24, p. 315).
The deposit of bones found in a corner of room 7 of the Archives Complex probably represent the remains of a single episode of burned animal sacrifice and large-scale feasting that occurred shortly before the palace was destroyed.
Feasts of this sort are likely to have played a critical role in Mycenaean society.
The bones may have been brought to room 7 in order to verify to palace authorities that a sacrifice had been completed.

Vitruvius ' probable social status had an effect on the contents of the « De architectura ».
The education proposed for the architect, the receipt of a wage, and pleasure all shape the treatise.
A close reading of the cameo appearance of Aristippus in Book 6, praef.
6 shows that Vitruvius arguably uses the figure of this pleasure-loving philosopher - offered to the reader as a stand-in for the architect - to focus and negotiate further the issues of status, pay, and pleasure.

Oedipus ' solution to the riddle of the Sphinx (as described by Athenaeus 10, 456 B) may be read and used as an interpretive key to puzzle through the significance of Odysseus ' encounters with the monsters of Od. 12, in particular the journey between Scylla and Charybdis (12, 85-97 ; 232-259 ; 441-458).
The riddle focuses on the problem of the relationship of body parts - hands, feet, and voice - to the human creature as a whole.

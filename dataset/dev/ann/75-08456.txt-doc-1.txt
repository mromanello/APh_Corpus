The original core of the House of Vestals should be dated to the late 3rd cent.
B. C. The House of Vestals underwent at least three major phases of remodeling : the integration of earlier properties into a single house in the mid-1st cent.
B. C. ; the addition of piped water and mosaics in the A. D. 20s ; the final changes after the earthquake of A. D. 62.
The constructional history of the House of Vestals allows some insights into the development of key features of Roman domestic architecture, such as the atrium and peristyle.
It also yields evidence for how Pompeian society worked.
In particular, it shows that the differentiation of a social elite was expressed in domestic architecture from the early stages of urbanization.

Examines verses 81-86 of Martial 's poem, which mentions Deucalion and Pyrrha before enumerating a second « catalogue of sins », focusing on the apparent contradiction between Martial 's virulent opening attack on mythology and his own recourse to mythological themes.
Also studies the intertextuality with Ovid 's Metamorphoses 1, 313-415, and relations between the genres of satire and epic in general

 An analysis of Terence's « Andria » reviews the Roman rhetorical approach to the initial « narratio » (49-168), then works beyond the limitations of the rhetorical comments to interpret Terence's dramatic accomplishment : the creation of a persona in the narrating father Simo, who loses the sympathy of the audience by his evident failures as a father.

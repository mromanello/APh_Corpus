Includes discussion of the use of Greek in Late Antique Syria and Palestine. 
 The use of Greek prevailed where the Hellenized elite held sway in Palestine, Southern Syria and Transjordan, while Aramaic was more prevalent in North Syria and Mesopotamia. 
 Considerations on the translation of Greek scientific and philosophical texts from Greek via Syriac to Arabic under the Abbasid dynasty (8th-10th cent. A.D.)

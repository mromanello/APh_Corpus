M. Hengel argued (=> 57-04546) that the Gospel of Mark was written in Rome in the year 69 ; he was convinced that more should be made of the Year of Four Emperors, a period of civic chaos and warfare. 
 Analysis of the role of the emperor as a potent force in the religious, civic, political, and military unity of Rome and her gods ; the all-pervasive Jerusalem Temple ; and Josephus' return to Rome in 71 and the publication of the « Jewish War » shows that these themes overlap with Mark's Gospel and are consistent with Rome being the place of composition. 
 B. Incigneri (=> 75-05641) dates the Gospel two years later, arguing persuasively for a critical role of the triumph of Vespasian and Titus in 71 as the catalytic event for Mark

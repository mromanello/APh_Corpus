On the « Praeparatio »'s narrative of descent beginning with the Phoenicians and Egyptians and culminating with the Greeks (1, 9, 19-2, 6, 10), its strategic role as ethnic argumentation and its apologetic force. In the « Praeparatio », Eusebius offers a description of Christianity as distinct from the nations of the world that will shape readers' ethnic sensibility in such a way that they will grant legitimacy to Christianity ; hence, his defense of Christianity lies in its very conception as an ethnos, and the polemic against pagan religion lies in its embodiment within the histories of the other nations.

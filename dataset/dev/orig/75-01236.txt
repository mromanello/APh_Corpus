Focuses on the meaning the poem may have had for a contemporary audience. Attis is a « gallus », a castrated devotee of the Mater Magna, and thus a reconstruction is attempted of the mental picture that Romans of Catullus' time had of « galli ». Special importance is given to Lucretius' excursus on the Mater Magna, and his interpretation of castration as a punishment for lack of « pietas ». Finally, Vergil's « Aeneid » is used to suggest that Catullus' poem may also have been read as participating in a discourse about Roman national identity

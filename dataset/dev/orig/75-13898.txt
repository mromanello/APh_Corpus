The writings of Hippocrates (Epid. 1 ; Aph. 3-4 ; Aer. 10) ; Celsus 3, 3, 4, 1-3 ; and Galen, « De morborum temporibus » in particular record descriptions of periodic fevers that correspond closely to the known varieties of malaria during Greco-Roman times. Malaria was well established as a serious disease in the Eastern Mediterranean of the 5th cent. B.C.

Studies the extension which, according to Plato, the activity of « aisthesis » should receive.
Although human « aisthesis » is antepredicative, it is nevertheless apparent in doxic judgement.
Distinguishing objects means for « aisthesis » involves the mysterious presence of those « common » somethings mentioned in the « Theaetetus ».
We cannot determine whether « aisthesis » can name its objects.
« Aesthetic experience » should be translated as « perceptual experience »

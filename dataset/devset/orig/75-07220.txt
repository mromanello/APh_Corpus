Some of the vulgarisms in texts from Late Antiquity transmitted by a manuscript tradition may belong to a later period than the one in which the text was originally composed, and some peculiar features in the literary texts of this period may indirectly reflect the changes in the colloquial language. Both kinds of texts are therefore of value when we try to make an assessment of the linguistic situation in the later centuries of the Roman period. Here, the text of Egeria is used as a case study : her language is compared to what we find in more literary texts from the same period

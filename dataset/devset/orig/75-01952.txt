Offers a comparative analysis of the topos of the « lion's share » in the three classical fables. In order to identify the peculiarities and implications in Phaedrus' version, focuses on the divergences of the three texts, the poets' applications of the moral issues expressed in the « fabula docet » lines, and the immanent conflict of nature and culture (« physis » and « nomos ») in the two main spheres of the fable

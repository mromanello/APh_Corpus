Aristotle is right when he says that if, after impregnation by a first male, a second male copulates with a hen while the eggs are forming, the whole brood will take after the second cock (GA 730 A 7-9 and 757 B 2-3 ; HA 560 A 9-14). 
 Poultry breeders have been aware of the phenomenon since the later 1920s.

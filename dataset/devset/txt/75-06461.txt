Introduction to a discussion of the poetics of deixis in Greek Lyric. 
 Investigation of the poetics of deixis begins with an exploration of linguistic forms that point to diverse kinds of objects : extra-textually to realia in the implied context ; backward and forward to objects within the text ; and imaginatively to objects brought into existence by the act of pretending to designate them. 
 Rhetorical questions about the poetic uses and effects of deixis are posed.

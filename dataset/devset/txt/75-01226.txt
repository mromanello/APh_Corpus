Offers a survey and analysis of the Hellenistic elements in Catullus 63. 
 After a description of the poem's Hellenistic background it is argued that in this poem Catullus makes a selective, but careful and creative use of Hellenistic elements, with a certain focus on the means of allusion and « Kreuzung der Gattungen ». 
 In doing so he alerts the reader to the poem's position within the literary tradition and within his own corpus. 
 The meta-poetic aspects of his treatment of Hellenistic elements may require further exploration

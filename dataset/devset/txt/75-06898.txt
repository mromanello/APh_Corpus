In the context of the Picus-Zeus narrative, one of the last representatives of Christian Euhemerism from the 4th cent. 
 A.D. (=> 74-08403), traces the sources of the story of the Assyrian origin of Cronus and Picus-Zeus, followed by their westward migration. 
 Comparison with the story of Cadmus in Nonnus' « Dionysiaca », ultimately based on the « Aeneid », shows that this account belongs among the number of mythological « topoi » which could be exploited in composing a new narrative. 
 Virgil's account, in turn, was transmitted to Nonnus by way of Pisander of Laranda's 60-book mythological compendium entitled « Heroicae Theogamiae », written under Alexander Severus

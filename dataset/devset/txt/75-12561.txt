Two aspects of the winter season depicted in Roman and late antique art, namely its productivity and its festive side, are investigated, as well as some of its religious customs. 
 A personification with attributes is the favored artistic form, although other imagery, including vignettes of rural life and images of laboring « putti » and of baskets of fruit are also found, especially in the media of mosaic pavements and marble sarcophagi.

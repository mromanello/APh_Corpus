The cycle of the wall-paintings in the Villa of the Mysteries outside Pompeii includes imagery generally recognized as being connected with Dionysiac rites and marriage. 
 Although the ways in which the two groups of imagery work together may not be fully understood, it is certain that they were intended to work together as an integrated whole. 
 That the paintings represent certain sacred rites undertaken by the lady of the house during the spring festivities of Anthesteria is an idea worthy of further consideration.

« Works and days » cannot be reductively thought of as a didactic presentation to the Greek farmer. 
 Analysis of Op. 414-503 in terms of epic forms suggests the development of a literary figure : the plowing discussed there stands for the human condition. 
 The lines establish the ideal addressee's identity as someone engaged in productive activity, and not so much a part of a theological order as of a world that includes gods, as opposed to someone engaged in helter-skelter pursuit of transitory activities, perhaps war specifically.

Readers of the « Rhetoric » need to explore Aristotle's other works to understand his « metarhetoric », that is, his notion of the prior knowledge a rhetor needs to have in order to be rhetorical. 
 Examination of statements made at Mem. 449b 10-452a 17 demonstrates how an understanding of Aristotle's general theory of human action can enhance our comprehension of the « Rhetoric »

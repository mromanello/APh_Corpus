Critics of C. 1, 3 have long perceived it as a problematic conflation of propempticon and diatribe on the folly of navigation. 
 In fact the poem contains a four-fold shift of meaning that is missed if the two-part structure is assumed. 
 In addition, Vergil's fourth « Eclogue » pervades the ode verbally and thematically, and in a way calculated to flatter Vergil even as Horace emulates him on poetical and political levels.

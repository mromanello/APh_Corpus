Isidore, orig. 8, 9 presents a classification of the diverse group of ritual experts he calls « magi ».
Exploration of this section 's structure, sources, and contents sheds light on how Isidore 's account matches the practices of magic and divination in his own day.
Isidore 's chapter is about the kinds of practitioners that could be grouped under the term « magi ».
Isidore classified these figures mainly by measuring the degree of demonic influence they exhibited, but also by secular standards, especially technical distinctions in their aims and methods.

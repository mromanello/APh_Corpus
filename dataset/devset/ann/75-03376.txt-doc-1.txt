The history of the debate over the authenticity of the « Octavia » remains worth recalling, both for its interest as an early example of philological sleuthing and because, ever since Coluccio Salutati articulated the problem in 1371, the play 's dubious authorship has discouraged discussion of the text.
The importance of the « Octavia » lies in its interest as a contemporary, or at least near-contemporary, Roman account of the last years of the Julio-Claudian principate.

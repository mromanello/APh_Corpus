A text linguistic analysis of the narrative structure of Catullus 63 attempts to provide a better insight into this poem as a narrative.
It is observed that the « narrative event structure » is in fact subservient to the « thematic structure » of the poem, in which the devices of contrast and repetition play an essential role.
The all-pervasive use of repetition in the poem is then elaborated upon, and the implications of this observation for its interpretation, leading up to the conclusion that in Catullus 63 repetition is a theme (or aim) in itself

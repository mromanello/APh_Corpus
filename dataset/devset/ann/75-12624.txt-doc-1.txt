On : the range of symbolic associations surrounding the relief sculpture of Demokratia crowning the Athenian Demos that accompanied the law proposed by Eukrates against the establishment of tyranny (SEG 12, 87) ; the various communities and individuals who invested in it ; and the role of personifications in political allegory.
This analysis shows both the potency of personifying representations of the Athenian people and the interpretive complexities that they create.

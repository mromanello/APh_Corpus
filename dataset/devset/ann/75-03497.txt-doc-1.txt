In Ovid 's « Metamorphoses », Achilles ' function is to set up a common heroic element for all the episodes of the Trojan War that are narrated or alluded to.
Analysis of the episodes that concern him (12, 70-145 and 580-619 ; 13, 441-448 and 576-622) demonstrates that we should avoid defining him simplistically as a merely negative figure.
Actually, the structure of the narration should be perceived as a series of independent scenes.
Unity is given by the recurring theme of fame.

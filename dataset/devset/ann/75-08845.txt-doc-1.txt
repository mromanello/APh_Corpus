On the archaeological evidence for convivial practices in Bronze Age Crete in comparison with the material indications of feasting on the Greek mainland.
Through comparison of Mycenaean evidence and two large LMIIIB-C pottery assemblages at Phaistos, discrepancies as well as reciprocal contributions in traditions of Minoan and Mycenaean feasting are evident.
The evidence from Phaistos demonstrates that communal drinking and feasts in LMIIIB-C Crete were celebrated to facilitate social communication and promote ideological strategies and political activities

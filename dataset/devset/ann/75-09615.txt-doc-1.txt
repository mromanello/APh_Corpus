Discussion of supplication on Roman coins has overestimated prosopographical and political factors and underestimated religious ones.
Coins such as the denarius minted by M. Aurelius Scaurus in 58 B. C. (ANS 2002.46.492) express conventional religious values and serve the purpose of promoting the minter or other political figures by the way in which the supplication is depicted.
This depiction depends on the interplay of religious scruples and the structure of supplication, a practice with four distinct steps.

Respect for others and for oneself is the main theme of the « Octavia ».
The play looks to tragedy for its types, particularly in the roles of nurses and male confidants.
The double, competing chorus is a feature of several plays of Euripides and some of Seneca 's.
The absence of the death of one of the main characters links the « Octavia » most to the late plays of Euripides (« Medea » and « Trojan Women »).

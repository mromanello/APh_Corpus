1, 34 and 5, 17 Augustine defends a vision of Romulus ' asylum initiative as described in Servius ' commentary on the « Aeneid » (ad. Aen. 2, 761 and ad Aen. 8, 342), but he subordinates it to an eschatological perspective on the history of the City.
The asylum of Romulus is only a preliminary phase in the emergence of the heavenly city, an image of an institution that will attain its perfection in the city of the elect.
In the pagan approach, as in the Christian one, the genesis of the City conditions the subsequent course of its history (for the German version => 75-00841)

Discussion of the archaic gold and silver coins that were buried in the great audience hall (the Apadana) and found in the 1933 excavations of the University of Chicago Oriental Institute at Persepolis (IGCH 1789).
These coins were symbols of Darius I 's control of western sources of wealth and leading centers of western economic activity.
An interpretation of the coins as issues of states already under Persian sway by the time of the foundation of Apadana need not imply a date as late as the 490s for the foundation of that building.

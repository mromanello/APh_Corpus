Response to the arguments of C. A. Bobertz (=> 61-01220) concerning Roman patron-client relationships and the extent to which such a social pattern existed in the relationship between Cyprian and his church in the early years of his episcopacy.
In the period between Cyprian 's conversion in about 248 and the synod of bishops in 251, which was Cyprian 's response to the problems brought about by the Decian persecution, the interplay between the scriptural injunctions to care for the needy without expecting return and the Roman system of patron-client relationships, coupled with the threats both to Cyprian 's leadership and to the financial security of wealthier Christians brought about by the persecution, resulted in dramatic upheavals.

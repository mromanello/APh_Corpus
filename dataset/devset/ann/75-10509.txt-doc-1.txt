The Athenian treasury at Delphi is a material trace of city politics in the 480s.
This function may be tracked through the monument 's iconography ; its use of marble from the medizing island of Paros ; its relation to the Alkmeonid temple of Apollo ; and the responses it evoked at Delphi and in Athens.
Special attention must be given to the methodological problem of finding meaning in non-iconic or non-representational features such as building materials.
A new reading of Pindar, P. 6 encapsulates what was at stake in this building project.

Examination of Plato 's effort to comprehend the problem of the unity of motion and rest, change and permanence, the Many and the One, the particular and the universal, in the world of our experience leads to the conclusion that we are justified both in speaking of Plato 's genuine Heracliteanism and in seeking the origins of Platonism as a philosophical tradition in Heraclitus (frs. 1 ; 2 ; 12 ; 17 ; 34 ; 49 A ; 60).

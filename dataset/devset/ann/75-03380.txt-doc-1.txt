The role of allegorical deities and the significance of deification are two aspects of « Octavia » that warrant closer attention.
« Roma », « Fortuna », « Iustitia », « Fides », and « Pietas » are central figures in the play.
In the conversation between Seneca and Nero (437-592), the issue in dispute is deification, the means by which the historical is transformed into the divine.
